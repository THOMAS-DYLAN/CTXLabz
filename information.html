General
Foundation
What Is a Research Peptide?
Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins. Research peptides are synthetic compounds studied in laboratory settings for their biological activity.
Handling
Reconstitution
Most lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides require reconstitution with bacteriostatic water before use in a research setting. Proper technique ensures stability and accurate concentration.
Storage
Storage Guidelines
Peptides are sensitive to heat, light, and humidity. Lyophilized peptides should be stored at −20°C. Once reconstituted, refrigerate at 2–8°C and use within 28 days.

Growth Hormone Peptides
GHRH Analogue

A GHRH analogue that stimulates pulsatile growth hormone release. Research examines its role in visceral adipose reduction and IGF-1 axis regulation.
GH Secretagogue
Ipamorelin
A selective ghrelin receptor agonist. Research focuses on pulsatile GH release with minimal effect on cortisol and prolactin compared to other secretagogues.
Long-Acting GHRH
CJC-1295
A long-acting GHRH analogue studied for extended half-life via Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) technology and sustained GH/IGF-1 elevation in preclinical models.
Combination

A combined GHRH analogue and secretagogue. Research examines synergistic GH pulse amplification — the GHRH analogue sets the pulse frequency, ipamorelin amplifies the release.
